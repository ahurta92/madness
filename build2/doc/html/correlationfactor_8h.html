Detailed Description

class for regularizing singular potentials in the molecular Hamilton operator

Introduction

The correlation factors are intended to represent the cusps (nuclear or electronic) in the molecular wave function. Their commutator over the kinetic energy operator give a potential operator that cancels the singular potential

[T, f12] = U + 1/r12 R^-1[T, R] = U_nuc - sum_A Z_A/r1A

The regularized potentials U and U_nuc contain two terms each, a local potential, (denoted U2), and a potential that is multiplied with the derivative operator (denoted U1)

U = U1 . ( - ) + U2 U_nuc = U1 . + U2

with

U2 = (^2 f12) U2 = R^{-1}(^2 R)

U1 = ( f12) U1 = R^{-1}( R)

To construct a nuclear correlation factor write:

std::shared_ptr<NuclearCorrelationFactor> nuclear_correlation = create_nuclear_correlation_factor(world,*calc);

where calc is an SCF calculation which holds the molecule and the nuclear_corrfac parameter name.
